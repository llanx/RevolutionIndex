**Variables to source (catalog numbers from variable-catalog.md):**

1. **#1 Income/Wealth Inequality** (Strong, Contested, fed-data)
   - Known measures: Census ACS Gini (SIPOVGINIUSA on FRED), WID top 1% income share (sptinc992j), Fed DFA wealth shares (WFRBST01134), Census income by quintile

2. **#2 Real Wage Growth / Labor Share** (Strong, fed-data)
   - Known measures: CES0500000003 (avg hourly earnings), MEHOINUSA672N (median household income), PRS85006173 (labor share)
   - Use `fred_search` for "real wage" and "labor share" to discover alternatives
   - Use `fred_series_info` to verify each candidate

3. **#5 State Fiscal Distress** (Strong, Contested, fed-data)
   - Known measures: Treasury debt_to_penny, interest_expense endpoints; GFDEBTN (federal debt on FRED), FYFSGDA188S (surplus/deficit % GDP)
   - Use `query_fiscal_data` to verify Treasury endpoints
   - Use `fred_series_info` for FRED series

4. **#6 Financial Crisis / Systemic Stress** (Strong, fed-data)
   - Known measures: STLFSI4, NFCI, DRTSCILM (bank lending), TEDRATE
   - Use `fred_series_info` to verify STLFSI4 and NFCI status (already verified in research but re-confirm)

5. **#8 Elite Overproduction** (Strong, fed-data partial)
   - Known measures: WID top 1% income share, advanced degree holders (Census B15003), JOLTS job openings
   - Document the Georgescu education-job mismatch proxy construction recipe: Census advanced degrees / BLS professional job openings
   - Use `census_search_variables` to find education attainment variables
   - Use `bls_search_series` for JOLTS series

6. **#9 Unemployment Rate** (Strong, fed-data)
   - Known measures: UNRATE, U6RATE, LNS14000006 (Black unemployment), SLUEM1524ZSUSA (youth)
   - Use `fred_series_info` to verify each

7. **#10 GDP Growth Rate** (Strong, fed-data)
   - Known measures: A191RL1Q225SBEA (real GDP growth), GDP (nominal level)
   - Use `bea_gdp_national` to verify BEA data availability

8. **#14 Relative Deprivation / Expectation-Reality Gap** (Moderate, fed-data)
   - Constructible from: MEHOINUSA672N (actual) vs. UMCSENT (expectations proxy)
   - Document construction recipe

9. **#16 Housing Affordability** (Moderate, fed-data)
   - Known measures: MSPUS (median sales price), MORTGAGE30US, price-to-income ratio
   - Use `fred_search` for "housing affordability" and "price to income"
   - Use `hud_fair_market_rents` to check HUD data

10. **#17 Inflation Rate** (Moderate, fed-data)
    - Known measures: CPIAUCSL (all items), CPIFABSL (food), CPIENGSL (energy)
    - Use `bls_cpi_breakdown` to discover CPI component series
    - Use `fred_series_info` to verify

11. **#18 Consumer Confidence / Sentiment** (Moderate, fed-data)
    - Known measures: UMCSENT (Michigan), CSCICP03USM665S (OECD, discontinued)
    - Document the CSCICP03USM665S discontinuation explicitly
    - Recommend handling: drop weight from FSP ETI and redistribute, since Conference Board CCI is not free on FRED and UMCSENT creates PLI data overlap
    - Use `fred_search` for "consumer confidence" to check for any new alternatives

12. **#27 Household Debt / Leverage** (Moderate, fed-data)
    - Known measures: HDTGPDUSQ163N (household debt to GDP), TDSP (debt service ratio), REVOLSL (revolving credit)
    - Use `fred_series_info` to verify each

13. **#40 Cost of Living Pressure (Composite)** (Moderate, fed-data)
    - Constructible from: CPI food + shelter + energy + medical components
    - Document construction recipe using BLS CPI breakdown
    - Use `bls_cpi_breakdown` to enumerate component series

For each variable entry, include:
- Catalog number and variable name (matching variable-catalog.md exactly)

- Do NOT use UMCSENT as a replacement for CSCICP03USM665S in FSP (violates zero-overlap design)
