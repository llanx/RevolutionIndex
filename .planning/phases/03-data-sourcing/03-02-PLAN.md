**Variables to source (8):**

1. **#3 Political Polarization (Congressional)** (Strong, other-data)
   - Primary: VoteView DW-NOMINATE scores (CSV download from voteview.com/data)
   - Document: House and Senate polarization (party mean distance), 1st-119th Congress (1789-2027), weekly DB dumps
   - Specific files: `HSall_members.csv` (member-level), `Sall_members.csv` (Senate), `Hall_members.csv` (House)
   - Alternative: Use `congress_house_votes` and `congress_senate_votes` MCP tools to verify congressional voting data availability for bipartisan voting frequency metric
   - Note: DW-NOMINATE is the gold standard for US congressional polarization measurement (McCarty et al. 2006)

2. **#4 Affective Polarization** (Strong, other-data)
   - Primary: ANES partisan feeling thermometer difference (CSV download from electionstudies.org)
   - Document: Cumulative Data File (1948-2020), Time Series studies (biennial/quadrennial)
   - Specific variables: VCF0218 (in-party thermometer), VCF0224 (out-party thermometer), difference = affective polarization
   - Alternative: Pew partisan antipathy surveys (published reports, irregular frequency)
   - Note: Biennial/quadrennial frequency -- tag as periodic survey data, use LOCF for alignment
   - This is a "short series" for the affective component (concept measured since ~1980, systematic since ~2012)

3. **#11 Elite Factionalism / Fragmentation** (Strong, other-data)
   - Primary: VoteView intra-party DW-NOMINATE spread (standard deviation of party members' scores)
   - Document construction recipe: SD of DW-NOMINATE 1st dimension within each party per Congress
   - Alternative: Use `fec_search_candidates` to explore primary challenge rates as proxy
   - Note: Constructible from VoteView data, not a pre-computed series

4. **#15 Horizontal Inequality (Between-Group)** (Moderate, fed-data)
   - Primary: Census ACS racial/ethnic income gaps (B19013B-I series for median household income by race)
   - Use `census_search_variables` to find racial income gap variables
   - Alternative: BLS earnings by race/ethnicity (CPS data on FRED)
   - Use `fred_search` for "median income by race" or "earnings gap"
   - Alternative: Chetty et al. Opportunity Insights data (other-data, CSV download)

5. **#19 Intra-Elite Wealth Gap** (Moderate, other-data)
   - Primary: WID.world top 10% vs top 1% income/wealth share ratio
   - Use `fred_series_info` on WFRBST01134 (top 1% net worth) and WFRBSB50107 (top 10% net worth) to verify Fed DFA series
   - Document WID download for income dimension
   - Construction recipe: (Top 1% share) / (Top 10% - Top 1% share) = intra-elite concentration ratio

6. **#20 Middle-Class Income Share** (Moderate, fed-data)
   - Primary: Census ACS income by quintile (middle 60% share)
   - Use `fred_search` for "income share quintile" or "middle class income"
   - Alternative: WID pre-tax income shares (P50-P90 group)
   - Document construction recipe if no pre-computed series exists

7. **#31 Anti-System Party Vote Share** (Moderate, fed-data)
   - Primary: FEC election results for third-party and anti-establishment candidates
   - Use `fec_search_candidates` to explore data availability for historical elections
   - Alternative: Dave Leip's Atlas of US Presidential Elections (other-data) for historical third-party vote shares
   - Document: Available for presidential elections (1789-present), midterms more complex
   - Note: Defining "anti-system" requires a coding decision deferred to Phase 4

8. **#45 Wealth Concentration (Top 0.1%)** (Strong, other-data)
   - Primary: WID.world top 0.1% wealth share (sptinc p99.9p100)
   - Use `fred_series_info` on WFRBSTP1300 (Fed DFA top 0.1% net worth share, quarterly, 1989-present)
   - Fed DFA is a strong alternative to WID for wealth (quarterly vs. annual, but shorter coverage)
   - Document both sources with tradeoff notes

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Do NOT treat VoteView data as API-accessible -- it is CSV download only (no REST API)
- Do NOT assume ANES questions are consistent across all waves -- document which waves contain which variables
- Do NOT include FEC data without noting that it covers financial data, not vote totals (MIT Election Lab or state SOS data needed for actual vote shares)
- Do NOT create weak/speculative proxies for variables without strong literature-validated mappings

Append Domain 3: Institutional / Democratic Quality to the inventory document. This domain is the largest gap in the existing 3 models (entirely uncovered per synthesis). Most variables source from V-Dem v15, making this section heavily dependent on a single dataset.

**Variables to source (8):**

1. **#13 Regime Type / Institutional Quality** (Strong, Contested, other-data)
   - Primary: V-Dem v15 Liberal Democracy Index (v2x_libdem), Polyarchy Index (v2x_polyarchy)
   - Document: CSV download from v-dem.net, v15 (March 2025), US 1789-present, annual
   - Specific V-Dem variables: v2x_libdem, v2x_polyarchy, v2x_regime, v2x_regime_amb
   - Alternative: Freedom House Freedom in the World scores (freedomhouse.org, annual, 1972-present)
   - Note Contested status: Walter (2022) vs. Svolik (2019) debate on US applicability. V-Dem codes US as full democracy throughout, making level variation minimal -- rate-of-change may be more informative.

2. **#21 Judicial Independence** (Moderate, other-data)
   - Primary: V-Dem v15 Judicial Independence index (v2juhcind, v2juncind)
   - Alternative: V-Dem Judicial Constraints on Executive (v2x_jucon)
   - Document V-Dem variable codes, coverage, measurement approach (expert survey)

3. **#22 Freedom of Expression / Media Independence** (Moderate, other-data)
   - Primary: V-Dem v15 Freedom of Expression index (v2x_freexp_altinf)
   - Alternative: Reporters Without Borders Press Freedom Index (rsf.org, annual, 2002-present)
   - Alternative: Freedom House Freedom on the Net scores
   - Document all three with tradeoff notes (V-Dem longest coverage, RSF most widely cited)

4. **#23 Legislative Constraints on Executive** (Moderate, other-data)
   - Primary: V-Dem v15 Legislative Constraints index (v2xlg_legcon)
   - Alternative: V-Dem Executive Respects Constitution (v2exrescon)
   - Note: For US, these indices have historically been near ceiling -- rate of change and sub-indicator movement more informative than absolute level

5. **#24 Electoral Integrity / Fraud Perception** (Moderate, other-data)
   - Primary: V-Dem v15 Clean Elections index (v2xel_frefair)
   - Alternative: Electoral Integrity Project (PEI) by Pippa Norris (Harvard Dataverse, expert survey, 2012-present)
   - Alternative: Grumbach (2023) State Democracy Index (state-level, subnational)
   - Note: Post-2020 election denial is a critical context -- survey data on fraud perception may supplement structural measures

6. **#29 Voter Access Restrictions / Partisan Gerrymandering** (Moderate, other-data)
   - Primary: Grumbach (2023) State Democracy Index (CSV, state-level, 2000-present)
   - Alternative: Brennan Center voter restriction tracking (published reports)
   - Alternative: Stephanopoulos & McGhee efficiency gap metric (computable from election returns)
   - Note: Primarily a state-level phenomenon -- national aggregation needed for composite indicator

7. **#32 Executive Aggrandizement** (Moderate, other-data)
   - Primary: V-Dem v15 Executive Index components (v2x_execorr, v2exrescon)
   - Alternative: Bright Line Watch expert surveys on democratic norms (2017-present, irregular)
   - Note: Bermeo (2016) identifies executive aggrandizement as the most common form of contemporary democratic backsliding

8. **#38 State Capacity / Institutional Quality** (Moderate, other-data)
   - Primary: V-Dem v15 Government Effectiveness components
   - Alternative: World Bank Worldwide Governance Indicators (WGI) -- government effectiveness, regulatory quality (annual, 1996-present)
   - Use `wb_indicator` MCP tool to check World Bank WGI data for "government effectiveness"
   - Alternative: Hanson & Sigman (2021) state capacity dataset (academic, coverage varies)

**V-Dem Documentation Requirements:**
For all V-Dem variables, document:
- Exact variable code(s)
- Measurement method (expert survey, factual, composite index)
- Scale range and interpretation
- Download location: https://v-dem.net/data/the-v-dem-dataset/ -> Country-Year: V-Dem Full+Others -> CSV
- File: V-Dem-CY-Full+Others-v15.csv
- License: Creative Commons Attribution 4.0
- Update frequency: Annual (typically March)
- Known limitation for US: Many indices near ceiling, making absolute levels less informative than within-country trends

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Do NOT treat V-Dem indices as interchangeable -- each has specific measurement methodology and scale
- Do NOT assume V-Dem captures recent US democratic erosion well -- the US is still coded as a liberal democracy, and the variation is subtle
- Do NOT rely solely on V-Dem for institutional quality -- combine with Freedom House, Bright Line Watch, and Grumbach for robustness
- Do NOT include Polity V as a source -- it is deprecated (final version 2018, no longer updated)
